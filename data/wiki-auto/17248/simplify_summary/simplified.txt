keflavik ( pronounced ’ , meaning ' driftwood bay ' , is a town in southwest iceland .
as of 2016 , its population is 15,129 .
in 1995 it merged with njarðvik and hafnir to form a municipality called reykjanesbær .
it was founded in the 16th century .
later , it grew from flight operations at the keflavik international airport .
the airport used to hold a significant nato military base .
it is iceland 's main international hub .
during world war ii the military airfield served as a refueling and transit depot .
during the cold war , naval air station keflavik was in control of marine and submarine traffic from the norwegian and greenland seas into the atlantic ocean .
air force forces were added to provide radar monitoring , fighter intercept , in-flight refueling , and aerial / marine rescue .
after the collapse of the soviet union , the base was cast into doubt .
the base officially closed on september 30,2006 , when the united states withdrew the remaining 30 military personnel .
in iceland , keflavik was known as a rich source of musicians during the 1960s and 70s .
the local geography is mostly fields of basalt rubble , with a few hardy plants and mosses .
on a clear day , one can see snæfellsjokull across the bay , some 115 km away .
the city of keflavik has cool summers and moderately cold winters .
there is no dry month but july is the month that gets the least amount of precipitation .
winter high temperatures are mild .
the average high of 13 ° c on average is july .
the town is represented in sports by iþrotta-og ungmennafelag keflavikur .
the former nato military base naval air station keflavik is used as a setting for an important story line in tom clancy 's novel " red storm rising " .
clancy described the base , the geography , local flora , and the station equipment .

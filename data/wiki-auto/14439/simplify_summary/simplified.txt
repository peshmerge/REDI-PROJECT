a harmonic series is a series of sounds , which are divided by sinusoidal waves .
pitched musical instruments are often based on an acoustic resonator .
at the frequencies of each vibrating mode , waves travel in both directions along the string or air column , reinforcing and canceling each other to form standing waves .
interaction with the surrounding air causes sound waves , which travel away from the instrument .
because of the typical spacing of the resonances , these frequencies are mostly limited to integer multiples , or harmonics , of the lowest frequency .
the musical pitch of a note is usually the lowest part of the note .
the musical timbre of a steady tone is strongly affected by the relative strength of each harmonic .

many pitched acoustic instruments are designed to have some parts that are close to being whole-number ratios with very low inharmonicity .
the piano is one of the most important musical instruments of western tradition .
other pitched instruments , especially certain percussion instruments , such as marimba , vibraphone , tubular bells , timpani , and singing bowls may contain mostly inharmonic partials , but may give the ear a good sense of pitch because of a few strong partials that resemble harmonics .
they may give no impression of implying any particular pitch .

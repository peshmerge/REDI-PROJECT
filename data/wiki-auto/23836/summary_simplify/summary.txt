He fought against these writings throughout his papacy, and in 400 he called a council to discuss them.
During his reign he also encouraged Catholics in North Africa to fight Donatism.
He instructed priests to stand and bow their head as they read from the gospels.
Jerome speaks of him as a man of great holiness who was rich in his poverty.
He died in Rome and was eventually buried in the Catacomb of Pontian together with his son, who would be Pope Innocent I, probably a unique case of a Pope being succeeded by his son.

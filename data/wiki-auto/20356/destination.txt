The Mozambique Channel is a part of the Indian Ocean between the island nation of Madagascar and southeast Africa, mostly the country of Mozambique.
It was the place of the World War II Battle of Madagascar.
The channel is about 460 km across at its narrowest point between Angoche, Mozambique, and Tambohorano, Madagascar.
The channel reaches a depth of 3,292 m (10,800 feet) about 230 km off the coast of Mozambique.

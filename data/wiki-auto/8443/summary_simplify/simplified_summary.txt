different techniques are used to find trees with a suitable hollow , including knowledge of landscape and termite activity patterns , and a kind of tap or knock test , in which the bark of the tree is peeled back , and a fingernail or the blunt end of a tool , such as an axe , is knocked against the wood to determine if the right resonance produces the right resonance .

the didgeridoo is played with continuously vibrating lips to make the drone while using a special way of breathing called circular breathing .
with practice , the player can sustain a note for as long as desired .
the second resonance of a didgeridoo is usually around an 11th higher than the fundamental frequency .
the vibration produced by the lips has harmonics , i . e . , it has frequency components falling exactly in the ratio 1 : 2 : 3 etc .
the harmonics of the fundamental note are not systematically assisted by instrument resonances .
at some frequencies , resonances of the voice cavity stop the oscillatory flow of air into the instrument .
these formants , and especially their variation during the inhalation and exhalation phases of circular breathing , give the instrument its readily recognizable sound .
other changes in the didgeridoo 's sound can be made by adding vocal sounds to the drone .
most of the vocal sounds are related to sounds emitted by australian animals , such as the dingo or the kookaburra .
to make these sounds , the players simply use their vocal cords to make the sounds of the animals whilst continuing to blow air through the instrument .
the results range from very high-pitched sounds to very low sounds .

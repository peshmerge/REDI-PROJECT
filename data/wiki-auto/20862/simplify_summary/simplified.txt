in a society , the conditions of land tenure underlie all social or economic factors .
there were two legal systems of landholding .
one was the system of holding land " allodially " in full outright ownership .
the other was a use of " precaria " or " benefices " .
to these two systems , the carolingian monarchs added a third , the aprisio .
the " aprisio " made its first appearance in charlemagne 's province of septimania in the south of france , when charlemagne had to settle the visigothic refugees who had fled with his retreating army after the failure of his zaragoza expedition of 778 .
he solved this problem by making a " desert " of uncultivated land belonging to the royal " fisc " under direct control of the emperor .

manorial administration involved significant expenses , perhaps a reason why smaller manors tended to rely less on villein tenure .
dependent holdings were held by agreement of lord and tenant , but tenure became in practice almost always hereditary , with a payment made to the lord on each succession of another member of the family .
villein land could not be abandoned , at least until demographic and economic circumstances made flight a viable proposition ; nor could they be passed to a third party without the lord 's permission , and the customary payment .
although not free , villeins were by no means in the same position as slaves : they enjoyed legal rights , subject to local custom , and had recourse to the law subject to court charges , which were an additional source of manorial income .
sub-letting of villein holdings was common , and labour on the demesne might be changed into an additional money payment , as happened from the 13th century .
like feudalism , together with manorialism , manorial structures were not uniform .
in the later middle ages , areas of incomplete or non-existent manorialization persisted while the manorial economy underwent substantial development with changing economic conditions .
not all manors had all three classes of land .
demesne accounted for approximately a third of the arable area , and villein holdings rather more .
the proportion of unfree and free tenures could vary greatly .
the proportion of the cultivated area in demesne tended to be greater in smaller manors .
the proportion of free tenements was generally less variable , but tended to be less important on the smaller manors .
manors varied similarly in their geographical arrangement : most did not coincide with a single village , but rather consisted of parts of two or more villages , most of the latter having also parts of at least one other manor .
this situation sometimes led to replacement by cash payments or their equivalents in kind of the labour duties of those peasants living farthest from the lord 's estate .

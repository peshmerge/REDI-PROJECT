miso , depending on the variety , consists of a starter culture called koji , soybeans , and usually a grainference of either rice , barley , or rye .
the miso goes through a two step process .
koji is made by introducing the mould " aspergillus oryzae " onto steamed rice .
it is isolated from plant matter whose fruit is usually rice or cultivated .
in the past , the natural presence of " a . oryzae " spores was made to create koji .
tane-koji is produced in the same way as koji .
it is an aerobic fungus and is the most active fermenting agents in koji .
amyloytic enzymes such as amylase aid in the breakdown of starch in the grains to sugar and dextrin , while protease catalyze the breakdown of proteins into smaller peptides or amino acids .
these both help in the digestion of the mixture of rice and soybeans .
depending on the strain of " a . oryzae " , enzymatic composition changes thereby changing the characteristics of the final miso product .
for example , the strain used to create the sweeter white miso would likely produce a higher content of amylolytic enzymes , while a soybean miso might have a higher content of proteolytic enzyme .
to create optimal conditions for enzymatic production and the growth of " a . oryzae " , the koji 's environment must be carefully regulated .
temperature , humidity and oxygen content are all important factors in not only maximizing mould growth and enzyme production , but to prevent other harmful bacteria from producing .
once the koji reaches a desirable flavour profile it is usually mixed with salt to prevent further fermentation .
although other strains of fungi have been used to produce koji , " a . oryzae " is the most desirable because of a number of properties , including the fact that it does not produce aflatoxin .

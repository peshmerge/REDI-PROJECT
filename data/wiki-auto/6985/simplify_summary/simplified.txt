chlorosis is a condition in which leaves produce insufficient chlorophyll , turning them yellow .
chlorosis can be caused by a shortage of iron or by a shortage of magnesium or nitrogen .
many plants are adapted to grow in soils with specific ph levels and their ability to absorb nutrients from the soil can depend on this .
chlorosis can also be caused by viruses , bacteria and fungal infections .

the chlorophyll maps show milligrams of chlorophyll per cubic meter of seawater each month .

the highest chlorophyll concentrations , where tiny surface-dwelling ocean plants are thriving , are in cold polar waters .
it is not the cold water that stimulates the phytoplankton .
instead , the cool temperatures are often a sign that the water has welled up to the surface from deeper in the ocean , carrying nutrients that have built up over time .
in polar waters , nutrients accumulate in surface waters during the dark winter months when plants ashore grow .
when sunlight returns in the spring and summer , the plants grow in high concentrations .
